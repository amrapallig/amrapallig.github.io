Story of two different diffusivities before mixing

Salt fingering is a mixing process, example of double diffusive instability, that occurs when relatively warm, salty water (red) overlies relatively colder, fresher water. It is driven by the fact that heated water diffuses more readily than salty water. These experiments are result of curiosity with small science experiments that I love to explore in my spare time. I conducted these fun experiments at Colorado State University.
